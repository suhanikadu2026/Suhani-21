Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj

About

Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj (1630–1680) was a legendary Maratha warrior-king and the founder of the Maratha Empire in Western India. Born at Shivneri Fort to Shahaji and Jijabai, he established Hindavi Swarajya (self-rule) against the Bijapur Sultanate and Mughal Empire using innovative guerrilla tactics (Ganimi Kava) and a strong navy. He was crowned in 1674 at Raigad Fort.

Achievements

Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj (1630–1680) was a visionary leader who founded the Maratha Empire, establishing Hindavi Swarajya (self-rule) against Mughal and Bijapur Sultanate dominance. He was a pioneer of guerrilla warfare, built a strong navy, created a welfare-based administrative system (Ashtapradhan), and promoted religious tolerance, becoming an enduring symbol of Indian resistance.

Legacy

Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj (1630–1680) left an enduring legacy as the founder of the Maratha Empire, pioneering guerrilla warfare (Ganimi Kava) and establishing Hindavi Swarajya (self-rule). He is revered for visionary leadership, progressive administration, religious tolerance, and creating a disciplined army, serving as a timeless icon of courage and justice in India.
